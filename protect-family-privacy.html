Why families are targeted

  • Children and older adults are frequently listed with addresses and relatives.
  • Exposure increases the risk of impersonation, scams, and unwanted contact.
  • Public listings can reveal routines, schools, and family connections.

How family exposure happens

People‑search listings

Public directories publish household members, addresses, and age ranges.

Broker networks

Data brokers share profiles across multiple sites and refresh them regularly.

Public record sources

Property records, court filings, and voter registrations can be re‑posted online.

Family-focused removal workflow

Household scan

Identify listings tied to parents, children, and older adults in the home.

Verified opt‑outs

Submit removals with identity verification and follow‑up tracking.

Ongoing monitoring

Rescan and re‑submit when data returns or spreads to new sources.
