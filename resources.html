Privacy resources & guides

Learn how your personal information ends up online and what you can do about it.

Understanding the problem

How data brokers work

Data brokers collect information from public records, social media, purchase histories, and other sources. They compile profiles and sell them to anyone willing to pay — including scammers and stalkers.

What information is exposed

Common data points include your full name, home address, phone numbers, email addresses, relatives' names, property records, and sometimes financial details.

Why it keeps coming back

Even after removal, data brokers frequently re‑list your information. Public records get updated, and brokers re‑scrape their sources on a regular cycle.

Steps you can take

1. Run an exposure scan

Start by understanding what's out there. Our free exposure scanner searches multiple engines to show what's publicly discoverable about you.

2. Submit opt‑out requests

Most people‑search sites have opt‑out processes, but they vary widely. Some require ID verification, mailed forms, or repeated follow‑ups over weeks.

3. Monitor and re‑remove

Removal is not a one‑time fix. Regular monitoring ensures that when data reappears, it gets removed again before it can be used against you.

4. Reduce future exposure

Review social media privacy settings, limit public records where possible, use a P.O. box for registrations, and be cautious about sharing personal details online.

Who is most at risk?

Law enforcement & military

Officers and service members face heightened risk when their home addresses and family details are publicly accessible.

Domestic violence survivors

Publicly listed addresses and phone numbers can put survivors in danger. Removal is a critical safety measure.

Public‑facing professionals

Executives, healthcare workers, judges, teachers, and others with public roles are frequently targeted using information from data brokers.

Anyone concerned about identity theft

The more personal data available online, the easier it is for identity thieves to build a complete profile and commit fraud.
