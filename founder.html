A Note From the Founder

Hardline Privacy was built because exposure risk became normalized while accountability did not. Families are routinely listed across broker networks, and most people are expected to navigate opaque removal systems on their own.

The standard here is straightforward: every removal workflow must be verifiable, every monitoring cycle must be intentional, and every client update must be clear enough to evaluate. Hype is not a substitute for operational follow-through.

Accountability means owning outcomes, not just submissions. That is why Hardline Privacy uses a human-verified model with review, follow-up, and escalation when exposure returns.
